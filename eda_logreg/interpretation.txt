The playoff qualification model has a high ROC AUC (≈0.9), indicating it is very effective
at distinguishing playoff from non-playoff teams, which aligns with the dominance of overall 
strength metrics like Net Rating. In contrast, the conference finals model has a lower 
AUC (≈0.7), showing only moderate separation ability. This drop reflects the increased 
uncertainty and competitive parity in the postseason, where top teams are harder to 
distinguish. 


For the precision-recall curve, the playoff qualification model looks extremely strong:
an average precision of about 0.99 is far above the 0.533 baseline,which means the model
is doing a much better job than chance at identifying which teams will make the playoffs
The conference finals model is much harder, with an AP of 0.538 versus a 0.250 baseline;
hat is still better than random, but it shows the task is much noisier and the model has a
harder time finding the rare true positives among playoff teams.

For the Calibration curve, the playoff qualification model has a very low Brier score of 0.039, 
which means its predicted probabilities are very close to the actual outcomes and can be 
trusted fairly well. The conference finals model’s Brier score of 0.188 is much worse,
showing that its probability estimates are less reliable and more affected by the uncertainty 
of deep playoff outcomes.
